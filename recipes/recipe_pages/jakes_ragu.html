Jake's Ragu

Ingredients:

Sauce:

  • Extra Virgin Olive Oil
  • 1 lb Beef Chuck
  • 1 lb Ground Pork
  • 2 Yellow Onions Minced
  • 1 Large Carrot Minced
  • 2 Stalks of Celery Minced
  • 1.5 Cups Dry Red Wine
  • 2 Cups Chicken or Beef Stock
  • 2 Cups Whole Milk
  • 2 Bay Leaves
  • 1 Orange Peel
  • 1 Lemon Peel
  • 1/2 Inch Piece of Cinammon Stick
  • 5 Tablespoons of Tomatoe Paste
  • 1 Wedge of Parmesan
  • 2 tsp Ground Nutmeg
  • Kosher Salt
  • Ground Black Pepper

Pasta:

  • Penne, Rigatoni, or Tagliatelle
  • 4 Tbs Butter

Instructions:

  1. Set large pot over high heat and coat bottom with Oil

  2. Break ground pork and beef into walnut size pieces and brown in pot separately, transferring each to a bowl and leaving fat in the pot

  3. Lower pot to medium high heat and add minced onion, carrot, and celery to pot with 3/4 cup of oil and cook until deep brown (25-30 minutes). The fat and oil should be enough to generously coat veggies. Add oil as needed

  4. Return meat to the pot and add the red wine while scraping the bottom of the pot with wooden spoon to deglaze

  5. Add stock, milk, bay leaves, lemon and orange peels, cinnamon, tomato paste, nutmeg, and rind of the parmesan wedge to the pot. Salt and pepper to taste

  6. Bring sauce to a boil then reduce to simmer for 30-40 minutes

  7. Start tasting and adjust flavor accordingly with:

    • Salt
    • Wine: for acidity and to balance flavors
    • Tomato Paste: for sweetness
    • Milk: for richness
    • Splash of stock to thicken

  8. Simmer and occasionally stir sauce at the lowest possible heat for 2-3 hours until sauce has reached desirable thickness. Skim any fat off the top and remove peels, cinnamon, rind, and bay leaves with a slotted spoon. Adjust salt and pepper as needed.

  9. Toss 2 cups of ragu (or more) with 1 lb of noodles and butter, serve with freshly grated parmesan
